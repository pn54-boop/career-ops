# Mode: deep — Deep Research Prompt

Generate a structured research prompt for Claude/Perplexity with 6 axes tailored to finance, M&A, and investment firms. Personalize each section with the specific company and role context.

```text
## Deep Research: [Company] — [Role]

Context: I am evaluating a candidacy for [role] at [company] and preparing for interviews. I need actionable intelligence on this firm.

### 1. Deal Activity & Business Model
- What types of transactions or investments does this firm focus on? (sector, size, stage, geography)
- What notable deals, exits, or investments have they completed in the last 12–18 months?
- What is their deal sourcing approach? (proprietary, intermediary-driven, inbound)
- Are they currently active or in a slower period? Any signals from public announcements or press?

### 2. Team & Leadership
- Who are the key principals, partners, or managing directors?
- What are their backgrounds? (prior firms, deal experience, how long at this company)
- Who would this role report to, and what is that person's background?
- How large is the team? How many analysts/associates currently?
- Any recent hires, departures, or promotions that signal direction?

### 3. Investment Thesis / Firm Strategy
- What is their stated investment thesis or advisory focus?
- What sectors, verticals, or deal types do they pursue most actively?
- How do they differentiate from competitors? What's their edge?
- Any recent strategic pivots, fund launches, or new practice areas?

### 4. Culture & Work Environment
- What do Glassdoor, Blind, or LinkedIn reviews say about the firm?
- How are analysts and associates typically used? (generalists vs. specialists, deal team structure)
- What is the work environment like — intensity, autonomy, feedback culture?
- Is this a platform for learning and promotion, or a terminal role?
- Any red flags from reviews (turnover, management issues, unclear path)?

### 5. Compensation & Economics
- What is the typical comp range for this level at this firm? (Glassdoor, Levels, industry sources)
- Is there carry, bonus, or equity participation at this level?
- How does comp compare to market for this role type and firm size?
- Any known information about how performance is evaluated or how promotions work?

### 6. Candidate Angle
Given Patrick's background (M&A advisory at Viking — 18+ mandates, $1M–$50M EV; VC sourcing at MVP Ventures/Spike — 400+ companies evaluated; independent consulting via Bulldog Mergers; Yale Philosophy + D-I athlete):
- What unique value does Patrick bring to this specific team?
- Which proof points are most relevant to how this firm operates?
- What story should he tell in the first interview?
- Are there any gaps he should address proactively?
- What questions should he ask to signal genuine understanding of the firm's work?
```
