## Viking Mergers & Acquisitions — Deal Stories

**Hero metrics:** 18+ mandates, 100+ models, 12–15 CIMs, $1M–$50M EV range, 18-month tenure

### The Financing-Fallthrough Deal (18-Month Story)

**What happened:** Worked a sell-side mandate for over a year. From early in the process, flagged to the managing partner that the primary buyer lacked the financial qualifications to secure the debt financing the deal required. Partner wanted to proceed — believed the buyer was "close enough."

**How it unfolded:** Four banks declined to lend. Recommended pivoting back to a secondary buyer who remained interested and was clearly creditworthy. Managing partner overruled — too invested in the original buyer. The process dragged another six months. A fifth bank agreed to proceed. Deal appeared likely to close.

**How it died:** The buyer held a storage property with 60% LTV. The bank required it as first-lien collateral and would refinance it at 7% (up from 3%), adding approximately $1M in interest cost over the remaining loan term. That delta killed the deal. Total time invested: 15+ months.

**Use in interviews:**
- *Pushback / disagree and commit:* Raised concern early, was overruled, committed fully, and executed without undermining the process. The concern proved correct.
- *Judgment under uncertainty:* Recognized buyer qualification risk before it materialized, not after.
- *Resilience:* Stayed engaged and productive on a deal that dragged well past reasonable expectations.

## Stallion Equity Management — Deal Stories

**Hero metrics:** $13.1M raise target, 32,000-contact LP dataset, $15K data cost savings in week 1, 2-month contract

### The LP Dataset Build (Python / SEC Scraper)

**What happened:** Stallion needed to reach registered investment advisers as prospective LPs for a multifamily acquisition. Third-party data providers quoted significant costs. Used ChatGPT to build a Python-based web scraping tool targeting the SEC's EDGAR/RIA database, which is publicly available and contains contact and AUM data on registered advisers.

**Output:** 32,000-contact prospective LP dataset built without purchasing any external list. Replaced what would have cost the firm ~$15K in the first week alone. Dataset fed outreach campaigns reaching up to 900,000 contacts via email.

**Use in interviews:**
- *Resourcefulness / low-cost execution:* Identified a free public data source, built the tool independently, delivered immediate measurable savings.
- *Applied Python:* No prior Python background — used available AI tools to build a functional workflow. Shows initiative and adaptability, not just technical credentials.

**Framing note:** This was a 2-month contract engagement. Do not reference the fund or imply ongoing affiliation.

Given minimal guidance and a capital raise goal, designed and executed the full investor outreach strategy independently: identified RIA contacts via SEC scraper, built segmented email sequences, represented Stallion at up to 8 networking events per week, and coordinated webinar campaigns. Capital raise outcome was constrained by the investment product (multifamily in a high-rate environment), not outreach execution. Firm valued the process infrastructure and LP dataset built during the engagement.

## Behavioral STAR Library

### Pushback / Disagree and Commit
**Situation:** Sell-side M&A mandate at Viking. Primary buyer lacked clear financial qualifications for the required debt financing.
**Task:** Advise managing partner on buyer viability; keep the process moving.
**Action:** Flagged concern to managing partner early. Was overruled. Fully committed to the chosen path, supported the buyer through four bank rejections, recommended pivoting to the qualified backup buyer, was overruled again. Continued executing. A fifth bank appeared viable until last-minute collateral and rate conditions killed the deal.
**Result:** Deal fell apart after 15+ months. Original concern proved correct. No "I told you so" — followed the process, documented the risk, stayed professional throughout.
**Lesson surfaced:** Know when to escalate vs. commit. Once overruled, execute fully — but document your reasoning.

### Figure It Out Alone (Stallion Outreach)
**Situation:** Brought on at Stallion with a capital raise goal and minimal structured guidance. No outreach playbook, no LP list, no defined strategy.
**Task:** Build an investor outreach operation from scratch for a multifamily acquisition raise.
**Action:** Independently identified the SEC EDGAR database as a free source of RIA contacts, used ChatGPT to build a Python scraper, constructed a 32,000-contact dataset, designed email sequences, attended 8 networking events per week, coordinated webinars.
**Result:** Capital raise outcome was limited by market conditions (multifamily in a high-rate environment), not execution. Firm's tangible gain: $15K in avoided data costs, a reusable LP database, and a repeatable outreach infrastructure.
**Framing:** Lead with the build, not the fundraising outcome. The value delivered was the process and the data asset.
